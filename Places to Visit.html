Edmonton Corn Maze

The Edmonton Corn Maze is a popular attraction located in Spruce Grove, Alberta, which is approximately 30 minutes west of Edmonton. The maze is cut into a field of corn that spans approximately 15 acres. The maze design changes every year, and this year’s design features the Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP) .The Edmonton Corn Maze offers a variety of activities, including the corn maze itself, jumping pillows, games, farm animals, potato cannons, and more . The farm also has a market that sells fresh produce and high-quality goods from Alberta food producers, artisans, and crafters .

University of Alberta Botanic Garden

The University of Alberta Botanic Garden is a 97-hectare (240-acre) property located in Parkland County, 15 minutes southwest of Edmonton, Alberta, Canada . It was established in 1959 by the University of Alberta and is the largest botanical garden in Alberta . The garden features cultivated gardens and plant collections, indoor showhouses, and extensive natural areas .If you’re planning to visit the garden, it’s open daily from 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM .

Elk Island National Park

Elk Island National Park is a national park in Alberta, Canada, that played an important part in the conservation of the Plains bison. The park is administered by the Parks Canada Agency. This "island of conservation" is 35 km (22 mi) east of Edmonton, along the Yellowhead Highway, which goes through the park. It is Canada's eighth smallest in area but largest fully enclosed national park, with an area of 194 km2 (75 sq mi). Visitors can enjoy hiking on park trails and guided tours, programs, workshops, and learning experiences. The park is open and accessible to visitors 24 hours a day, 365 days a year.

Fort Edmonton Park

Fort Edmonton Park is an outdoor living history museum located in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada. It is situated on 64 hectares (158 acres) of wooded parkland along Edmonton’s river valley . The park was established in 1967 as a Canada Centennial project to reconstruct the old Fort Edmonton . Today, it has grown to encompass much more and offers visitors a truly interactive and memorable experience .The park is open from Wednesday to Sunday from 12:00 PM to 4:00 PM . It features a variety of attractions, including a steam train ride, horse-drawn carriage ride, carousel, and midway games . Visitors can also explore the park’s four distinct eras of history: the fur trade era (1795-1821), the settlement era (1821-1891), the metropolitan era (1891-1914), and the post-metropolitan era (1914-1929) .

Art Gallery of Alberta

The Art Gallery of Alberta is a center of excellence for the visual arts in Western Canada, connecting people, art, and ideas . The museum is located at Churchill Square in downtown Edmonton and occupies an 8,000 square meters building 1. It was established in 1924 as the Edmonton Museum of Arts and was renamed the Edmonton Art Gallery in 1956 . The museum offers both in-person and virtual experiences.The museum is open on Wednesdays from 11:00 AM to 5:00 PM, Thursdays from 11:00 AM to 7:00 PM, Fridays from 11:00 AM to 5:00 PM, Saturdays from 11:00 AM to 5:00 PM, and Sundays from 11:00 AM to 5:00 PM .

West Edmonton Mall

West Edmonton Mall is a shopping mall located in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada. It is the largest mall in North America and the tenth largest in the world. The mall has over 800 stores and restaurants, 12 world-class attractions, and hotels. Some of the attractions include Galaxyland, which is the world’s largest indoor amusement park, World Waterpark, which is one of the world’s largest indoor water parks, and Marine Life, which is an aquarium that features over 100 species of fish1. The mall also has an ice rink, a miniature golf course, a bowling alley, and a movie theater.
